Our Mission

Valiant Heart Companions was founded with a simple yet powerful mission: to help survivors of sexual assault reclaim their lives through the healing bond of a trained service dog.

Our organization specializes in training dogs to perform specific tasks that mitigate the symptoms of trauma, including creating space in crowded environments, providing deep pressure therapy during anxiety attacks, and offering a comforting presence during nightmares and flashbacks.

We believe that every survivor deserves access to the unique support that service dogs can provide, regardless of their financial situation. That's why we provide our dogs at little to no cost to qualified applicants.

Survivor Stories

Every survivor's journey is unique. Here are some of their stories and how service dogs have been instrumental in their healing process.

Sarah's Journey to Healing

After experiencing sexual assault in college, Sarah struggled with PTSD, panic attacks, and severe social anxiety that made it impossible to continue her education.

Three years after being paired with her service dog, Cooper, Sarah has completed her degree and is now working as an advocate for other survivors.

Read Sarah's Full Story

Michael's Return to Life

As a male survivor, Michael faced unique challenges in finding support and resources. His hypervigilance and trust issues had isolated him from friends and family.

His service dog, Riley, has helped him navigate public spaces safely and reconnect with his community through a newfound sense of security.

Read Michael's Full Story

Elena's Path to Independence

After her assault, Elena developed agoraphobia so severe that she couldn't step outside her home alone. Simple tasks like checking the mail became overwhelming challenges.

With her service dog, Daisy, Elena has gradually reclaimed her independence, recently celebrating the milestone of traveling alone for the first time in five years.

Meet Our Dogs

Our service dogs undergo rigorous selection and nearly two years of specialized training before they're matched with survivors. Get to know some of our amazing canine heroes.

Ranger

Breed: Labrador Retriever

Special Skills: Deep pressure therapy, nightmare interruption, creating personal space in crowds

Ranger excels at sensing anxiety triggers before his handler even notices them, allowing for early intervention during potential panic attacks.

Learn More About Ranger

Lily

Breed: Golden Retriever

Special Skills: Room clearing, perimeter checks, grounding during flashbacks

Lily's calm demeanor makes her particularly suited for survivors who struggle with hypervigilance in new environments.

Learn More About Lily

Orion

Breed: Standard Poodle

Special Skills: Alerting to approaching people, boundary enforcement, medication reminders

Orion's intelligence allows him to adapt quickly to new tasks as his handler's needs evolve throughout their recovery journey.
